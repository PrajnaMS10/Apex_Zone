Shoulders and Abs Exercises

Overhead Press

ID: 17

Muscles Worked: Shoulders (deltoids), triceps, upper chest.

Instructions:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a barbell at shoulder height with an overhand grip.
  2. Engage your core and keep your chest up.
  3. Press the barbell overhead until your arms are fully extended.
  4. Slowly lower the barbell back to shoulder height.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Tips:

  • Keep the barbell close to your body throughout the lift.
  • Avoid arching your back; keep your core tight.
  • Keep your elbows slightly in front of your body during the press.

Lateral Raises

ID: 18

Muscles Worked: Shoulders (lateral deltoids).

Instructions:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand at your sides.
  2. Keep a slight bend in your elbows and raise the dumbbells out to the sides until they are at shoulder height.
  3. Lower the dumbbells back to the starting position.
  4. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Tips:

  • Keep your movements slow and controlled.
  • Avoid using momentum or swinging the weights.

Front Raises

ID: 19

Muscles Worked: Shoulders (anterior deltoids).

Instructions:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand in front of your thighs.
  2. Keeping your arms straight, raise the dumbbells in front of you to shoulder height.
  3. Lower the dumbbells back to the starting position.
  4. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Tips:

  • Maintain a slight bend in your elbows.
  • Perform the movement slowly and avoid swinging.

Reverse Flyes

ID: 20

Muscles Worked: Rear deltoids, upper back (rhomboids, traps).

Instructions:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand.
  2. Bend at your hips and knees, keeping your back straight and torso almost parallel to the floor.
  3. Let the dumbbells hang down below you with your palms facing each other.
  4. Raise the dumbbells out to the sides until they are in line with your shoulders.
  5. Lower the dumbbells back to the starting position.
  6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Tips:

  • Avoid swinging the weights; use controlled movements.
  • Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the top of the movement.

Plank

ID: 21

Muscles Worked: Core (abdominals, obliques), shoulders, back.

Instructions:

  1. Start in a forearm plank position with your elbows directly under your shoulders and your body in a straight line from head to heels.
  2. Engage your core and hold the position, avoiding letting your hips sag or rise.
  3. Maintain the position for as long as you can with proper form.

Tips:

  • Keep your neck in line with your spine by looking at a spot on the floor.
  • Breathe steadily and avoid holding your breath.

Hanging Leg Raises

ID: 22

Muscles Worked: Lower abdominals, hip flexors.

Instructions:

  1. Hang from a pull-up bar with an overhand grip and your arms fully extended.
  2. Keep your legs straight and together, and raise them to a 90-degree angle with your torso.
  3. Slowly lower your legs back to the starting position.
  4. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Tips:

  • Avoid swinging your body; use your core muscles to lift your legs.
  • Start with bent knees if you find the exercise too difficult initially.

Russian Twists

ID: 23

Muscles Worked: Obliques, abdominals.

Instructions:

  1. Sit on the floor with your knees bent and feet flat.
  2. Lean back slightly, keeping your back straight, and lift your feet off the ground if you can.
  3. Hold a weight (like a dumbbell or medicine ball) with both hands in front of your chest.
  4. Twist your torso to the right, bringing the weight towards the floor beside your hip.
  5. Twist back to the left, bringing the weight towards the floor beside your other hip.
  6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions on each side.

Tips:

  • Keep your movements controlled and avoid using momentum.
  • Focus on rotating your torso rather than just moving your arms.
